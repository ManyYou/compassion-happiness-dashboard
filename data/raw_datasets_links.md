# World Value Survey:
This source contains survey answers shared by 96221 respondents from 66 countries obtained between 2018 and 2023 about personal values or perceptions related to social trust, social norms, and political culture. The data is compiled by the World Value Survey which is an international research program backed by academics. The dataset is a 96221x613 tabular dataset (CSV or Stata) with spatial information on where the respondents come from. There are three types of attributes: categorical, ordinal, and numerical. 
Use: The values shared by the respondents that are related to the social aspects can be used to estimate compassion level across different countries. This information will help directly in answering Q1-Q3. 
Source: https://www.worldvaluessurvey.org/WVSDocumentationWV7.jsp

# OECD – Social Expenditure Database: 
This source contains information on the % of GDP that is spent on social welfare by OECD countries (38) between 1980 and 2022. The data is compiled by the Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development, which is an internationally recognized organization. The dataset is a 1701669x34 tabular dataset (CSV) with spatial information on the countries. There are three types of attributes: categorical, ordinal, and numerical. 
Use: The level of a country’s spending on social welfare indicates the level of social safety nets which is useful for Q2. 
Source: https://www.oecd.org/en/data/datasets/social-expenditure-database-socx.html


# World Happiness Report 2022: 
This source contains happiness scores from 146 countries in 2022. The score is calculated, compiled, and published by the Wellbeing Research Centre at the University of Oxford, UK. The dataset is a 146x12 tabular dataset (CSV) with spatial information on the countries. There are three types of attributes: categorical, ordinal, and numerical. 
Use: The happiness score is the main target variable for comparison with the compassion level for Q1-Q2.
Source: https://worldhappiness.report/ed/2022/

# World Bank – Gini coefficient: 
This source contains the Gini coefficient from 183 countries between 1960 and 2023. The data is published by the World Bank, which is a reputable international organization. The dataset is a 266x68 tabular dataset (CSV) with spatial information on the countries. There are two types of attributes: categorical and numerical. 
Use: Gini coefficient measures the level of inequality within a country which is useful for Q3. 
Source: https://data.worldbank.org/indicator/SI.POV.GINI

# World Bank GDP per Capita
A global dataset compiled by the World Bank, providing the GDP capita (a standard measure of population prosperity) for many countries between 1960 and 2023. 
Use: Measure the level of individual prosperity within each country 
Source:https://data.worldbank.org/indicator/NY.GDP.PCAP.CD
